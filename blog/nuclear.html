More about Nuclear reactors and the 3 Mile Island incident

10/11/24

This is a video about a true to scale nuclear reactor simulator which is located in Germany. The place it is housed is a museum of power plants which is the KSG GFS Simulator Center in Germany. This is an excellent piece for engineers planning to work in the nuclear field to study. The simulator actually has a mode for demonstrating what happened in the "Three Mile Island" incident from the last story. What happened at Three Mile Island was a valve malfunctioned causing pumps to shut down in the secondary water feeder. The water-feeder is a pump that flows cold water outside the U-tubes which enables the core water temperature to stay cool enough to continue to run. The reactor at this time reacts to the rising core temperatures (no pun intended) by dropping control rods into the core of the reactor. At the same time a relief valve on the reactor coolant pressurizer fails-open. Issues were piling up; misrepresented temperatures, valves stuck open, air bubbles and human error all caused boiling inside of the reactor coolant system. When water boils it evaporates causing the reactor's coolant levels to drop eventually leading to even more overheating. Soon the uranium rods were damaged from all of these issues releasing radioactive materials. If you are interested in learning more about the simulator, you can watch the video I have embedded here.
